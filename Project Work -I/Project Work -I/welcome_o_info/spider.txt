Spiders are air-breathing arthropods that have eight legs and chelicerae with fangs 
 able to inject venom. They are the largest order of arachnids and rank seventh in total
 species diversity among all other orders of organisms. 
Class: Arachnida
Order: Araneae; Clerck, 1757
Scientific name: Araneae
Kingdom: Animalia
Higher classification: Arachnid
Lifespan: Southern black widow: 1 – 3 years, Brown recluse spider: 1 – 2 years, Goliath birdeater: 15 – 25 years
